CURATION 2017-18

A Letter to You, the Reader

Although much of this year consisted of work, a large portion of my time was devoted to mini escapes through travel or exploring nature. Seth Godin, a prominent author and entrepreneur, advises to “set up a life you don’t need to escape from.” I agree with this; however, I enjoy spending time away to relax, but at the same time love learning at school. This year, I tried to create a balance through focusing on school or work when needed, and pursuing my passions on the side.

I discovered that we have a lot more free time than we believe. Everyone has 24 hours in a day, yet some let time drift away until there is none left, while others can make the most out of it. I’ve been making an effort to disconnect, as despite not owning a smartphone, when working on a project I tend to get carried away by the depths of the internet. We live in an age where technology is almost too accessible, making real experiences rarer and rarer.

Our society is so caught up in maximizing output that we often forget to take time for ourselves. For me, this is just the beginning, as I hope these mini travels lead to much larger ones in the future. Even locally, there are dozens of unknown territories created by nature waiting for me to find. Here’s to the adventures to come!
